\begin{abstract}
This whitepaper formally details the realization of the Universal Topological Quantum Computer (UTQC) structured by the UOR Atlas. By leveraging the content-addressed \textit{holospaces} substrate, the framework manifests a pointed topological-quantum modular tensor category (MTC) computationally. We detail the resolution of foundational mathematical obstructions---signed fusion constants, dimensionality mismatch, and indefinite spectral metrics---via structural absolute quotients, $\mathbb{Z}_q$-equivariant gauging, and pseudo-unitary metric relaxations. Exact computation over $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{24})$ decides the density question in both directions: on the unique 2-dimensional commutant block the projective image of the coupled generators is the finite projective Clifford group of exact order 24, so density in $SU(2)$ is refuted; on the 22-dimensional irreducible block the projective closure is dense in $PU(22)$, and on the 576-dimensional two-handle pair carrier it is dense in $PU(576)$, whence dense in $PU(24^{n})$ for every $n \ge 2$ by the classical two-local composition lemma---gate-level universal quantum computation on 24-dimensional-per-handle qudit carriers. The coherent abelian construction also natively yields a non-trivial entangling phase gate (Controlled-$Z$ equivalent) via double-braiding monodromy within the single consistent theory; because the exactly decided single-qubit image is Clifford, universality is carried by the qudit-carrier chain rather than by any $SU(2)$ gate set. Furthermore, we report a measured engineering property: content-addressed deduplication (UOR cache-collapse) over the finite modular sector, whose braid-group orbit plateaus at 32 distinct $\kappa$-addressed states in the repository benchmark, yielding large compute and memory deduplication for invariant evaluation---with no claim of subverting the $\#P$-hard tensor contraction boundary. Every exact claim is backed by a machine-checked certificate over $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{24})$; the remaining steps are classical representation-theoretic lemmas applied to those certificates, and the distinction is tracked explicitly throughout.
\end{abstract}
