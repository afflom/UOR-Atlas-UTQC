\section{Formal Verification of Universal Gate Sets}

The transition from a coherent topological space to an operational quantum compiler involves the synthesis of a universal quantum gate set. Within the UOR Atlas, the foundational operations---braiding and fusion---are constructed via explicit modular $S$ and $T$ matrices modeled over the MTC framework.

\subsection{The Modular \texorpdfstring{$S$}{S} and \texorpdfstring{$T$}{T} Matrices}
The modular $S$-matrix defines the topological S-transformation (a $\pi/2$ rotation) and encodes the mutual braiding statistics of the anyon classes, as well as the quantum dimensions of the objects $d_a = S_{0a}/S_{00}$~\cite{rowell2009}. The $T$-matrix encodes the topological twists (the phase accumulated by an anyon undergoing a $2\pi$ self-rotation). Together, the $S$ and $T$ matrices generate a projective representation of the modular group $SL(2, \mathbb{Z})$.

The formal validation infrastructure of this work rigorously tests the structural properties of these matrices. The computational suite programmatically asserts that the generated matrices strictly obey the required algebraic invariants:
\begin{align}
    S^4 &= I \\
    {(ST)}^3 &= S^2 \\
    \sum_c N_{ab}^c S_{cx} &= \frac{S_{ax}S_{bx}}{S_{0x}} \quad (\text{Verlinde formula})
\end{align}
where $N_{ab}^c$ are the fusion coefficients~\cite{verlinde1988}.

\subsection{The Braiding \texorpdfstring{$R$}{R}-Matrix and Yang-Baxter Equations}
Multi-anyon entanglement is algebraically enacted via the Braiding $R$-Matrix, which maps the fusion channel of two objects into their braided fusion channel. For the topology to remain consistent, the $R$-matrix must satisfy the Yang-Baxter equation~\cite{jimbo1989}:
\begin{equation}
    (R \otimes I)(I \otimes R)(R \otimes I) = (I \otimes R)(R \otimes I)(I \otimes R)
\end{equation}
